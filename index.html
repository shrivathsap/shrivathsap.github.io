Picture of the month

An Indian peacock shows off its brilliant plumage. To quote Darwin, the peacock might be "the most splendid of living birds" and I think we can all agree on that. There are two hypothesis on the evolution of these feathers: one is simply an attraction to the colours, another is the handicap principle which says that brighter, larger secondary characteristics, such as these feathers, indicate a fitter individual because non-fit ones couldn't afford them. The colours are not mainly due to pigments but due to optical effects from the nanostructures in the feather (see iridescence) and is a fascinating subject on its own. Here's a new word: peacocks are Gallinaceous birds. There is another species in the same genus - the Green peafowl, native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.
